📋  Why a Development Log?
Weekly development logs are standard practice in professional product teams — from startups to companies like Google, Spotify, and Tencent. Engineers and PMs write short, structured updates so the whole team knows what shipped, what’s blocked, and what’s next. Keeping yours concise and evidence-based is the skill.
This log is worth 20% of your grade. One entry per week, Weeks 3–9 (seven entries). Your pathfinder reads it to track progress.

🚀  Getting Started — Do This Now
This is a template. Do not edit this file directly.
1.Upload this file to your team’s XJTLU SharePoint.
2.Rename it: DevLog_[TeamName]_ENT208TC.docx  (e.g. DevLog_Phoenix_ENT208TC.docx)
3.Fill in the Team Information section on this page.
4.Share the link with your pathfinder and module leaders.
5.Delete this blue box and the yellow box above once your team has read them.
Each team creates one copy. Everyone in the team edits the same document.

⚠  Submission Requirement — Read This Now
You submit this document in Week 11 together with its SharePoint version history. The version history proves that you wrote entries week by week — not all at once before the deadline. Without it, your log cannot be verified.
This only works if you keep the file on XJTLU SharePoint from the start. Google Docs does not produce compatible version history. Moving to SharePoint later loses your earlier history. Start on SharePoint now.
Tip: Edit directly in SharePoint’s Word Online so every save creates a version automatically.
